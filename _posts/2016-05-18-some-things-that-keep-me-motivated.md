Look, this isn't always easy.

There are times when the appeal of a cold beer is pretty strong.  Sometimes it's because I'm angry.  Sometimes it's because I'm bored.  Sometimes it's because the sun is shining and its 70° F.  Sometimes, it's because it's Tuesday...

When the urge strikes, I find that it's good to remind myself why I got sober.  I find that it's good to look back on <a href="{{ '/soberboots/2015/10/28/its-taken-a-long-time-for-me-to-get-here/' | relative_url }}">some</a> <a href="{{ '/soberboots/2015/10/30/surrendering-to-saying-im-an-alcoholic/' | relative_url }}">older</a> <a href="{{ '/soberboots/2015/12/04/surrender/' | relative_url }}">posts</a> reminding myself of just where I was.  And sometimes, that's enough to set the mind on the right path.<!--more-->

Sometimes, I have to remind myself of all the good things that have happened as a result of getting sober.

<ol>
    <li><a href="{{ '/soberboots/2016/01/06/shame-insanity-and-awesome-sauce/' | relative_url }}">Freedom from Shame</a>.</li>
    <li><a href="{{ '/soberboots/2016/04/25/fishing-acceptance-happiness/' | relative_url }}">Learning to accept things as they are, some of the time</a>.</li>
    <li><a href="{{ '/soberboots/2016/04/23/conversations-and-connections/' | relative_url }}">Reconnecting with people who I care about</a>.</li>
</ol>

And sometimes, I need to remind myself that there are some really good valid reasons to keep at it.

<ol>
    <li>My health is improving - In fact, it's improved dramatically, my cholesterol and blood pressure are both down and my triglycerides dropped significantly (cut in half).</li>
    <li>I feel better most days.</li>
    <li>I've lost weight (not as much as I'd like, see item 10).</li>
    <li>I sleep soundly. Every. Single. Night.</li>
    <li>I no longer have a pain in my side (right where my liver is, btw).</li>
    <li>I no longer have IBS symptoms on a daily basis.</li>
    <li>I have more money to do things that I want to do -  I'm not spending it on booze.</li>
    <li>I'm much more present with my son. (And he notices it!)</li>
    <li>I can actually have a conversation with my wife without it turning into an argument (though that still happens too.)</li>
    <li>I can go to Safeway to buy ice cream at 9:00 PM  (because I'm not drunk as a skunk.)</li>
</ol>

I'm in a much better place than I was a day or two ago.  I won't lie and say that everything is peachy, but it sure ain't as bad as I felt yesterday morning.
