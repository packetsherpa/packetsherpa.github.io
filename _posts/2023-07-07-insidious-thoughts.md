<p>As a person who’s life could be described as one traumatic event after another, I’m really good at catastrophic thinking.&nbsp; I can plan for the worst with the best of them.&nbsp; I mean, I used to think about the fact that terrorists could hijack a plane and use it as a missile ever time I got on an flight long before 9/11.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>It’s a defense mechanism.&nbsp; A survival instinct.&nbsp; If I can imagine the worst case scenario, I can plan for it.&nbsp; And if I can plan for it, then it won’t be that bad.&nbsp; Except it doesn’t actually work that way.&nbsp; No matter how much you plan for the worst, when the worst happens you are still taken by surprise and it still sucks.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>So, it’s kind of hard to believe that it never once occurred to me that my brother would die before I did.&nbsp; Or that he’d die before he turned 50.&nbsp; I mean, it should have occurred to me.&nbsp; I have thought about every other person who is important to me dying before me – my parents, my wife, my friends, my son; but not once did it occur to me that Matt would die before I did.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>He’s been with me through all the tragedies.&nbsp; Every death that I’ve ever faced in my life, he’s been there.&nbsp; And now he’s not, because he died a week ago.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>I have never felt so alone, despite being surrounded by so many people, in my life. &nbsp; And I’ve found myself unable to believe that I never thought of it – as if thinking of it would have made it easier.&nbsp; I know it wouldn’t, and still I’m mad at myself for not thinking of it.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>This is how my brain works.&nbsp; This is what a lifetime of trauma and tragedy does to a brain.&nbsp; It warps your thinking.</p>



<p>I have been doing all the things that I know I need to do to take care of myself.&nbsp; I’ve been meditating.&nbsp; I’ve been talking about my feelings.&nbsp; I’ve been to meetings.&nbsp; I’ve made and taken phone calls.&nbsp; I went to a therapy appointment.&nbsp; I’ve examined the root causes of my uneasiness when others offer help.&nbsp; It’s clear to me that I’ve made a ton of progress over the past 7.75 years. I can identify the specific feelings that I have now – the ones that I didn’t understand in the past.&nbsp; The ones I drank over.</p>



<p>&nbsp;It’s also clear to me that the work never ends.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>This evening, I found myself enticed by an ad for some kind of special ice maker.&nbsp; The ad showed two rocks glasses, one with regular ice, and one with this super special clear ice.&nbsp; Bourbon was poured over the ice in each glass and the special ice seemed to completely disappear in the bourbon.&nbsp; I have to admit that the bourbon looked good for an instant.&nbsp; It was powerful – and then reality hit me.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>No romancing the drink.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>I had a bowl of ice cream instead.&nbsp; I’ll go to bed with a clear mind and a clear conscious and I’ll wake up feeling better.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
